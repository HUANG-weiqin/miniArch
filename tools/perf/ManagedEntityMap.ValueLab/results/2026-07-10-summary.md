## Observations

- `world.IsAlive(entity)` marginal cost is small in dense form: at 100k, RawDenseUnsafe TryGet hit is 10.3 ns/op vs CompetentDenseUser 11.8 ns/op and ProtoMap 12.4 ns/op.
- Dense arrays beat dictionary lookups for hot hit/remove paths: at 100k, ProtoMap TryGet hit is 12.4 ns/op vs CompetentDictionaryMap 59.0 ns/op; Remove is 13.1 ns/op vs 106.0 ns/op.
- ProtoMap is not meaningfully better than a competent dense user for hot lookups. At 100k, ProtoMap Get/TryGet/Remove is within noise of CompetentDenseUser; Set is slower (24.6 ns vs 16.4 ns) due to extra count/null/max-touched bookkeeping.
- `_maxTouchedExclusive` has clear cold-path value: at 100k, ProtoMap TrimExcess is 1.28 us vs CompetentDenseUser 70.18 us; at 1M, ProtoMap TrimExcess is 3.80 us vs 572.87 us. Clear is also faster (100k: 19.90 us vs 39.22 us; 1M: 748.77 us vs 1.528 ms).
- 100k-slot Align is cold-path acceptable: ProtoMap Align is ~2.6 ns per mapped slot, roughly 0.26 ms for 100k slots in this harness.
- Hot operations report 0 Gen0/1/2 and near-zero retained memory deltas. Large dense arrays are retained capacity by design; Clear removes references but does not release arrays, TrimExcess does.
